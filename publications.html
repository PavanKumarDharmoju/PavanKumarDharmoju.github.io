Virtual CRISPR: Can LLMs Predict CRISPR Screen Results?

S Song, A Abdrabou, A Dabholkar, K Day, P Dharmoju, J Perera, et al.

Proceedings of the 24th Workshop on Biomedical Language Processing, 354-364, 2025

This paper explores the potential of Large Language Models to predict CRISPR screen outcomes, representing a novel intersection of AI and genomics research. Our work investigates whether transformer-based models can understand genetic perturbation effects, potentially accelerating CRISPR-based drug discovery and functional genomics research. The study demonstrates promising results in predicting gene knockout effects using contextual biological knowledge encoded in LLMs.

Ranking System For All The Tourism Related Hotel Industries Using NLP and ML Approach

B Agarwal, SRG Reddy, PK Dharmoju, RK Mishra

2022 13th International Conference on Computing Communication and Networking Technologies (ICCCNT), 2022 • 1 citation

Developed an intelligent ranking system for tourism-related hotel industries combining Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning techniques. The system analyzes customer reviews, hotel amenities, and service quality metrics to provide comprehensive rankings. This work addresses the challenge of information overload in the hospitality sector by providing data-driven insights for both travelers and hotel operators to make informed decisions.

Forecasting Electrical Demand for the Residential Sector at the National Level Using Deep Learning

PK Dharmoju, K Yeluripati, J Guduri, K Palle

2021 International Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Machine Vision (ICAIMV), 2021 • 4 citations

Presents a novel deep learning framework for national-level residential electrical demand forecasting. The research develops advanced neural network architectures that incorporate temporal patterns, seasonal variations, and socioeconomic factors to predict electrical consumption. This work contributes to energy grid optimization and sustainable energy planning by providing accurate demand predictions that enable efficient resource allocation and grid management strategies.
